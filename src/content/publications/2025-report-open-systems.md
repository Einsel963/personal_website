This research note collects introductory materials and simple models related to quantum measurement, measurement backaction, and effective open-system dynamics. The focus is on developing physical intuition for how idealized quantum information-theoretic structures are modified by monitoring, decoherence, and environmental coupling.

Topics include weak and continuous measurement, quantum trajectories, Lindblad-type master equations, and non-Hermitian effective descriptions. This note is part of my broader preparation for studying measurement-induced dynamics and open many-body quantum systems.
