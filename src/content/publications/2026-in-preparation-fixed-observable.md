This work investigates quantum realizability questions for constrained Bell-type correlation data, with particular interest in settings where the local observables are fixed or otherwise restricted. Instead of focusing only on the maximal violation of a Bell expression, the project studies the geometry of correlation structures that can be generated by quantum states under prescribed measurement constraints.

The current focus is on extending ideas from the two-party CHSH scenario to tripartite Bell scenarios, especially the \((3,2,2)\) setting. The work aims to clarify the relation between fixed-observable constraints, tensor representations of correlators, and the stratification of correlation sets associated with fully separable, biseparable, and genuinely multipartite entangled resources.
