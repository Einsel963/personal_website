This report summarizes my ongoing study of tripartite Bell scenarios using
correlation tensors and spectral methods. The main setting is the \((3,2,2)\)
Bell scenario, with emphasis on Mermin and Svetlichny-type inequalities,
biseparable reductions, and the distinction between fully separable,
biseparable, and genuinely multipartite entangled states.

A central theme is the relation between the tensor structure of three-qubit
correlations and the geometry of Bell-inequality violations. In particular,
GHZ-family states provide a useful testing ground for understanding how
equatorial tensor components contribute to Svetlichny violations, while polar
components such as $T_{zzz}$ play a different geometric role.
