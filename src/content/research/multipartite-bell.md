My current research focuses on multipartite Bell scenarios and the geometry of quantum correlations. Starting from the conceptual structure of the CHSH scenario, I am interested in how the problem becomes richer in tripartite settings, where locality, separability, and entanglement admit several inequivalent layers.

A central object in my current work is the three-party Bell scenario, especially the \((3,2,2)\) setting. In this framework, I study how fully separable, biseparable, and genuinely multipartite entangled states give rise to different correlation structures. Bell-type inequalities such as the Mermin and Svetlichny inequalities provide useful probes, but they do not by themselves exhaust the geometry of quantum-realizable correlations.

Technically, I use correlation tensors and their spectral structure to analyze three-qubit states. For example, in GHZ-family states, the flattened correlation tensor reveals how equatorial components dominate the maximal Svetlichny violation, while the polar $T_{zzz}$-type component plays a different role. This motivates a more systematic study of how entanglement classes, measurement choices, and Bell-inequality geometry interact.

More broadly, I hope to understand multipartite nonlocality not only as a violation of inequalities, but also as a geometric and structural problem about the set of correlations that can be generated by quantum states and measurements.
