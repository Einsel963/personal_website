This direction studies multipartite Bell scenarios from both structural and
operational viewpoints. A central goal is to understand which families of
correlations can arise in many-party settings and how symmetry, marginal
constraints, and measurement choices shape the resulting correlation sets.

Questions in this area naturally connect to nonlocal games, entanglement
witnessing, and the search for informative inequalities beyond the bipartite
case.
